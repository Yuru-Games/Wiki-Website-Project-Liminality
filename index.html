Project Liminality : Game Design Database

Welcome to the technical design documentation for Project Liminality. This database logs all game statistics, numerical impact values on the Health Points (H.P.) and Sanity Bar (S.B.), as well as the behaviors of documented entities.

Select a category from the index to display the project's certified data.

ENTITY 01 : The Life Form

Entity Data
Danger Level5 Stars / Keter Class
StructureBlack cables / Organic tissue
AttributeBlind

Description & Behavior

The Life Form is a massive organism composed of black cables and organic tissue. The entity is completely blind, but patrols corridors searching for movement.

It has extremely sensitive hearing and easily detects sprinting, jumping, and other loud actions. It uses loud screams to locate its prey.

Offensive Mechanics

  • Echolocative scream: Blurs players vision, causes ringing sound in the ears, helps locate nearby players.
  • Tendril Grabs: Medium Ranged grab that it uses to crush you in the ground or to slam you on walls.
Counter-play: Crouch, avoid sprinting, stay quiet (QTE to breath), and use distractions when possible (throw items around to lure it).

ENTITY 02 : Skin-Stealers

Entity Data
Danger Level5 Stars / Keter Class
SpeedVery slow
MechanicInstant Absorb / Mimicry

Description & Behavior

Skin-Stealers are a gigantic fleshy organism. They spend most of their life motionless, silently observing their surroundings.

While very slow, they slowly move toward nearby prey. They instantly absorb you upon contact.

Mimicry Ability

Once a victim is absorbed, the entity takes on the exact appearance of the latest victim.

Counter-play: Keep your distance, be cautious around unknown players, and avoid Flesh-Residues.

ENTITY 03 : The Infection

Infection Data
Danger Level4 Stars / Almost Safe
TypeParasitic fungal organism

Infection Sources

The parasitic fungal organism is spread through contaminated organic matter and:

  • Black liquid puddles
  • Flesh residues
  • Dead bodies
  • Contaminated areas

Symptoms Progression

  • 20-40% : Hallucinations begin. Black tendrils/cables/tentacles appear on screen edges.
  • 40-60% : Arms and legs begin to transform. Visible mutations appears.
  • 60-80% : Severe hallucinations. Torso becomes infected.
  • 80-99% : Near-complete conversion.
  • 100% : Host becomes a Wretch.

Cure: Almond water or Royal Rations.

ENTITY 04 : Wretches

Entity Data
HostilityPassive
StateWeak and exhausted

Description & Behavior

Wretches are former humans completely consumed by The Infection.

Their bodies became entirely black as the fungal organism replaced and corrupted their original tissues. They are covered in fungal growth and have distorted proportions, though human features partially remain visible.

Despite their disturbing appearance, Wretches are not naturally hostile. They are most often found isolated, curled up in corners of empty rooms, quietly crying or remaining motionless for long periods of time.

Cure

You can cure them by pouring ALMOND WATER or ROYAL RATIONS on them. They then become a passive NPC that helps you after you cured them.

ENTITY 05 : Bone Thieves

Entity Data
Danger Level3/5 Stars
BehaviorHostile when spotting prey

Description & Behavior

Small scavenger-like creatures that inhabit rare regions of the Complex. Their elongated limbs they use to crawl around and their pleasant demeanors might fool you into thinking they're safe when they REALLY aren't. Once they detect you, their "Mouth" will open to reveal the ACTUAL beast inside of them.

Normally passive and avoids lighted areas. Frequently scavenges corpses and remains. Becomes hostile when spotting a prey, charging at extreme speeds once provoked.

Abilities

  • Bone rush: A sudden high-speed charge designed to close distance almost instantly.
  • Skeletal Extraction: Attempts to tear bones directly from the victim's body causing severe damages.
Counter-play: Stay aware of your surroundings. Dodge sideways when it charges. Use obstacles to block its path.

ENTITY 06 : Smilers

Entity Data
Danger Level4/5 Stars
HabitatDark zones

Description & Behavior

Hostile entities that inhabit dark environments. They are recognized for their glowing eyes and teeth forming a bright smile, hence their names. They never leave dark areas and they can appear in any region of the Complex.

Remains hidden in darkness, watches nearby wanderers, and attacks players entering dark zones. Avoids brightly lit areas.

Abilities

  • Shadow-swarm: They trap their victims and eat them.
Counter-play: Use your flashlight to kill it. Avoid entering dark rooms without checking thoroughly.

ENTITY 06-B : Frowners

Description & Behavior

A passive variant of the smiler. Frowners are rarely encountered and display none of the aggression shown by their counterparts.

Passive, avoids conflict, usually hides from wanderers, and is often found alone.

Abilities & Counter-play

Abilities: None Recorded.

Counter-play: Leave them alone, don't corner them.

ENTITY 07 : Hounds

Entity Data
Danger Level5/5 Stars
BehaviorHighly Aggressive

Description & Behavior

Fast-moving predators with distorted humanoid anatomy and animalistic behavior. Hounds are among the most bestial and hunting entities encountered in the Complex.

Highly aggressive, hunts moving targets, roams corridors searching for prey, and rarely abandons pursuit.

Abilities

  • Pack mentality: Multiple Hounds may coordinate to chase down prey.
Counter-play: Break line of sight. Use doors and obstacles. Remain quiet when possible. Never engage directly.

ENTITY 08 : Facelings

Entity Data
Danger Level1/5 Stars
BehaviorPassive

Description & Behavior

Humanoid entities lacking facial features. Most Facelings are completely harmless and show little interest in wanderers. Some have even been observed offering supplies to survivors.

Passive, wanders aimlessly, may approach players, and occasionally gives a random item.

WARNING: Some Skin-stealers have been known to imitate facelings in order to deceive wanderers.

Counter-play

Approach cautiously, watch for abnormal behavior, and never fully trust an unfamiliar faceling.
